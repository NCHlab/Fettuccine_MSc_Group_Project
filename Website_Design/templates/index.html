Welcome to Fettuccine Human LINE-1 & HERV Transposable Elements Platform

This platform provides complete and updated information about the predicted translated products of the Endogenous Retrovirus (HERV) and Long Interspersed Nuclear Elements (LINE-1) retrotransposons families and the powerful "pep-seq identifier" tool

“Transposable elements (TE) are segments of DNA that can move, or transpose, within the genome” : A) DNA Transposons: Direct transfer to a new genome location (cut-paste procedure), B) Retrotransposons: Copy their sequence to a new genome location using an RNA intermediate molecule.

Long Interspersed nuclear elements (LINE-1 or L1) are autonomous non-Long Terminal Repeat retrotransposons comprising around 17% of the human genome.

The LINE-1 elements are transcribed to an intermediate RNA molecule, which is translated into ORF1 and ORF2 proteins. These two proteins form a complex with the RNA which has the ability to integrate the RNA molecule at a genome location through reverse transcription.

Human Endogenous Retrovirus (HERV or ERV) sequences belong to the group of Long Terminal Repeat (LTR) retrotransposons corresponding to around 8% of the human genome.

For HERV elements, the existence of four genes (gag, pol, pro, and env) surrounded by the 3’ and 5’ LTR regions is crucial to their activity. Gag and Pol genes encode a protein complex which mediates the production of a cDNA molecule which can integrate the retrotransoson sequence into a new genome location.
